\section{Introduction}\label{sec:system_analysis_introduction}

This chapter presents the system analysis of Eduverse by defining the
operational problem space, summarizing the adopted analysis method,
organizing the functional and non-functional requirements, and documenting
the use-case and activity models that explain system behavior.

\section{Problem Domain and Operational Gaps}\label{sec:problem_domain_operational_gaps}

Eduverse addresses the operational gap that emerges when educational
institutions rely on separate tools for class administration, communication,
learning materials, assignments, examinations, live sessions, and results.
Such fragmentation weakens continuity across the learning process, increases
context switching for teachers and students, and makes it difficult to govern
academic workflows within one role-aware institutional environment.

The problem is intensified by multi-organization operation and differentiated
responsibilities, which require controlled membership, permissions, and
visibility boundaries. The same environment must also accommodate AI-assisted
learning support and programming support without relying on detached external
services.

\section{Analysis Methodology Adopted}\label{sec:analysis_methodology_adopted}

The analysis adopted for Eduverse was based on the implemented and validated
project scope rather than on speculative features. It proceeded through the
following activities:

\begin{itemize}
  \item reviewing the implemented web platform and the MVP mobile companion in
    order to identify the actual service boundaries;
  \item identifying the needs of the organization owner, teacher, and student
    as the principal stakeholders;
  \item organizing recurring educational workflows into functional modules
    that reflect the operational structure; and
  \item modeling system behavior through use-case and activity diagrams to
    relate scope, actors, and workflow progression.
\end{itemize}

\section{As-Is Analysis}\label{sec:as_is_analysis}

Before Eduverse, institutions commonly combine messaging applications,
file-sharing services, live-meeting tools, assessment utilities, and manual
follow-up practices. This arrangement separates class communication from
materials, submissions, results, coding practice, and contextual support,
which produces inconsistent records and slows coordination across roles.

\section{To-Be Analysis}\label{sec:to_be_analysis}

Eduverse proposes a unified, role-aware environment centered on the class
context and coordinated through organization membership and controlled access.
The web application serves as the full management environment, while the
mobile application remains an MVP companion for selected daily actions;
automated notifications, AI support, and IDE support are integrated without
expanding the mobile scope beyond that role.

\section{Requirements Engineering}\label{sec:requirements_engineering}

The requirements of Eduverse are derived from the problem-domain analysis, the
validated project scope, and the needs of the principal stakeholders. They
are stated at the module level so that the system scope is clear without
repeating every action already represented in the use-case diagrams.

\subsection{Functional Requirements}\label{subsec:functional_requirements}

\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{A. Authentication and Access:} Eduverse shall support public
    entry, account registration, login and logout, verification-email
    dispatch, and organization or role selection when multiple eligible
    contexts exist.
  \item \textbf{B. Organization and Role Management:} the platform shall allow
    organization owners to create organizations, apply feature presets, manage
    members and invitations, and assign or remove roles.
  \item \textbf{C. Class Management:} the platform shall support class
    creation, update, teacher assignment, membership control, and feature
    configuration.
  \item \textbf{D. Course Content and Materials:} the platform shall support
    material upload with metadata, viewing, search, filtering, download, and
    controlled deletion.
  \item \textbf{E. Class Communication and Notifications:} the platform shall
    provide class messaging and event-driven notifications with viewing,
    read-state management, and follow-up actions.
  \item \textbf{F. Assignment Management:} the platform shall support
    assignment authoring, publication, student submission through the allowed
    response modes, instructor grading, and feedback visibility.
  \item \textbf{G. Exam Management and Integrity:} the platform shall support
    exam authoring, optional passcodes, student attempts, integrity
    monitoring, review decisions, grading, and optional result release.
  \item \textbf{H. Results and Dashboards:} the platform shall provide
    role-specific dashboards and controlled access to class, assignment, and
    examination results, including detailed views where permitted.
  \item \textbf{I. Live Sessions and Whiteboard:} the platform shall allow
    teachers to start and end sessions and eligible participants to join,
    communicate, and use whiteboard features.
  \item \textbf{J. AI Learning Support:} the platform shall provide
    class-context AI assistance for explanation, summarization, drafting
    support, and bounded assessment-related support without replacing teacher
    judgment.
  \item \textbf{K. Integrated Coding Lab / IDE Extension:} the platform shall
    support environment selection, file operations, code editing, execution,
    terminal use, preview, and problem or console viewing for
    programming-oriented learning.
  \item \textbf{L. Profile and Help:} the platform shall allow users to manage
    profile-level preferences, switch active roles where permitted, and
    consult searchable help content.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Non-Functional Requirements}\label{subsec:non_functional_requirements}

\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Security and role-based access control:} access to
    organizations, classes, files, assessments, and live-session resources
    shall remain protected through authenticated, permission-controlled use.
  \item \textbf{Privacy:} academic records, messages, submissions, and
    results shall be handled with restricted visibility.
  \item \textbf{Reliability:} the platform shall preserve correct state for
    memberships, class records, materials, submissions, and examination data.
  \item \textbf{Usability:} core workflows shall remain understandable to
    organization owners, teachers, and students without unnecessary context
    switching.
  \item \textbf{Real-time responsiveness:} live sessions, chat, notifications,
    and related synchronous interactions shall provide timely updates.
  \item \textbf{Maintainability:} the system shall remain modular enough to
    support continued evolution of features such as AI support, live sessions,
    and IDE functionality.
  \item \textbf{Scalability:} the platform shall remain suitable for multiple
    organizations, multiple classes, and growing volumes of academic records
    and uploaded files.
  \item \textbf{Cross-client compatibility:} the mobile companion shall remain
    consistent with the permission and data model of the web platform while
    preserving its narrower scope.
  \item \textbf{Performance:} dashboards, files, results views, and real-time
    features shall perform at a level appropriate for regular educational
    operation.
\end{itemize}

\section{Use Case Modeling}\label{sec:use_case_modeling}

Use-case modeling expresses Eduverse through the interactions between the
principal actors and the services available to them. The diagrams are the main
analytical artifacts in this section, so the accompanying text notes only the
main modeling relationships.

\subsection{Actors and Modeling Scope}\label{subsec:use_case_actors}

The principal human actors of Eduverse are summarized in
Table~\ref{tab:eduverse_use_case_actors}. Their permissions are role-aware and
organization-scoped, which means that the same individual may encounter
different functional boundaries depending on the active organization and role.

\begin{table}[htbp]
  \centering
  \small
  \renewcommand{\arraystretch}{1.15}
  \setlength{\tabcolsep}{5pt}
  \caption{Principal Eduverse Use-Case Actors}
  \label{tab:eduverse_use_case_actors}
  \begin{tabular}{|>{\raggedright\arraybackslash}p{0.23\textwidth}|>{\raggedright\arraybackslash}p{0.57\textwidth}|}
    \hline
    \textbf{Actor} & \textbf{Analytical Role in the System} \\
    \hline
    Organization Owner & Governs the organization workspace, manages memberships and roles, configures features and extensions, oversees classes, and monitors organization-level dashboards. \\
    \hline
    Teacher & Manages instructional workflows such as classes, materials, communication, assignments, exams, grading, live sessions, AI-assisted support, and IDE-enabled learning activities. \\
    \hline
    Student & Participates in the learning process by joining classes, accessing materials, communicating, submitting assignments, taking exams, reviewing results, joining live sessions, using AI support, and using the IDE where the class enables it. \\
    \hline
  \end{tabular}
\end{table}

The mobile companion does not introduce a new human actor, and the AI layer
is treated as a supporting service rather than as a primary actor.
